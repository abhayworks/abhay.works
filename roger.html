Roger

Connecting shippers and carriers in the bulk freight trucking space.


Client

Bushel's long-standing client Roger

Context

Roger is an early-stage startup in the bulk freight trucking space that originally began as a project within Scoular, a multinational agricultural supply chain company. The startup is developing a mobile and web-based platform that connects shippers with carriers/drivers to improve efficiency throughout any load’s lifecycle by reducing paperwork and accelerating payment processes.

Challenge

The client determined that introducing text-based communication capability is yet another way in which interaction amongst all parties (carrier, driver, shipper), could be made more efficient. This determination was predicated on the discovery that all communication between parties was conducted within disparate mediums (phone, text, email, etc.) which resulted in a lack of continuity amongst users’ communication.

To foster communicative continuity, we needed to design a cross-platform messaging system while being constrained by “out-of-box” Sendbird functionality.

NB: This particular client was too UX-averse for its own good - likely due to not fully grasping what UX is and their penchant for corner cutting whenever possible, despite our best, incessant effort to educate. As a result, we were unable to perform much of the necessary research required to inform our design decisions. Fortunately, we were able to conduct an informal comparative analysis and remote guerrilla usability testing sessions with non-technical Bushel employees to mitigate the effects of the minimal research performed during the project’s early phases. This experience further instilled the importance of maximizing, and subsequently trusting my design instincts as well as making the most of research already performed for a pervasive feature - instant messaging.
